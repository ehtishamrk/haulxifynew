Developer Setup

How to Connect This Form to Google Forms + Apps Script

Follow these steps once to wire up form submissions to a Google Sheet and enable auto-reply emails. No server needed — runs entirely on Google's free infrastructure.

1

Create the Google Form

Go to forms.google.com → New Form. Add fields: Name, Email, Phone, Company, Industry, Fleet Size, Services Needed, Message, Plan Interest. Link it to a Google Sheet (Responses tab → green Sheets icon).

2

Get your Form ID and Entry IDs

Your form URL looks like: docs.google.com/forms/d/YOUR_FORM_ID/viewform. To find entry IDs: open the form, click the 3-dot menu → "Get pre-filled link", fill each field, copy the URL, and note the entry.XXXXXXXXX values for each field.

3

Update the JavaScript below with your IDs

In contact.html, find the GOOGLE_FORM_CONFIG object in the script block and replace FORM_ID and each entry.XXXXXXX with your real values. The form will then POST directly to Google Forms silently.

4

Add Apps Script for auto-reply emails

In your linked Google Sheet, go to Extensions → Apps Script. Paste the auto-reply script (provided in js/forms-appscript.gs). Set a trigger: On form submit → sendAutoReply. This sends a confirmation email to every lead automatically.

5

Test end-to-end

Submit a test entry through the form on your website. Check that it appears in the Google Sheet, that you receive a notification email, and that the submitter receives an auto-reply. Done — fully functional backend, zero hosting cost.
